Athanasias

by Plato

SOCRATES: Why have you come to me so late in the day, dear Athanasias?

ATHANASIAS: I want to know how to achieve immortality!

SOCRATES: Really? Why?

ATHANASIAS: I want to be a god! I want the power to rival Zeus!

SOCRATES: But is such a thing a noble goal?

ATHANASIAS: Screw nobility! I want power!

SOCRATES: Okay, then let me tell you how to achieve immortality, since you so relentlessly insist upon it.

ATHANASIAS: Finally! Thank you. I swear, if you teach me how to become immortal, I will never ask for anything else again.

SOCRATES: Yes, you've been quite needy lately. But here is the secret to immorality: Love your neighbor as yourself.

ATHANASIAS: What!? That's not an answer. There has to be an actual answer! You can't just give me a fake out.

SOCRATES: Can't I?

ATHANASIAS: Stop answering my questions with questions! Just tell me straight out how to live forever.

SOCRATES: Fine. Lean in closer, and I will whisper it to you. I can't have everyone in the world finding out how to live forever. I must parcel out this knowledge one bit at a time.

ATHANASIAS: Sure! Alright. I'm bending over, and I'm listening. Just waiting for you tell me how to live forever.

SOCRATES (whispering): No one gets to live forever, you annoying brute. And that is why I must kill you now!

ATHANASIAS: Ahhh! You're stabbing me! Damn my knowledge! It has led me to my doom.

SOCRATES: I hope you learned a valuable lesson, dear Athanasias.

ATHANASIAS: I did. And now I must die.

SOCRATES: And that's why people should never bother me late in the day. I just want to enjoy a good sunset for once. Geeze!
